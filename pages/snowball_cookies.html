Snowball Cookies

Snowball Cookies are a delightful holiday treat known for their buttery, crumbly texture and signature powdered sugar coating. These bite-sized cookies are made with simple ingredients like butter, powdered sugar, and finely chopped nuts, creating a melt-in-your-mouth experience. Resembling little snowballs, they’re perfect for festive gatherings, gift-giving, or a cozy snack with a warm beverage. Their irresistible sweetness and delicate texture make Snowball Cookies a timeless favorite for celebrations and everyday indulgence.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (226g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup (90g) confectioners’ sugar
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 cups (281g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt (omit if using salted butter)
  • Optional: 3/4 cup (100g) finely chopped toasted pecans
  • Coating: 1 1/4 cups (150g) confectioners’ sugar

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, cream the butter until smooth. Add confectioners’ sugar and beat until combined. Mix in vanilla extract.
  2. On low speed, gradually add the flour and salt until the dough comes together. Stir in pecans, if using.
  3. Chill the dough for 30 minutes.
  4. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  5. Roll the dough into 1-tablespoon balls and place them on the baking sheet, spaced 2 inches apart.
  6. Bake for 15 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden. Let cool for 5 minutes.
  7. Roll the warm cookies in confectioners’ sugar and let them cool completely. Roll again for a snowy finish.
  8. Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week.
