Mississippi Pancakes

Mississippi Pancakes are a hearty and flavorful twist on traditional pancakes, inspired by the comforting, soulful flavors of the South. Known for their rich texture and buttery golden edges, these pancakes are often made with a blend of cornmeal and flour, giving them a subtle, earthy sweetness and a slightly crispy exterior. Infused with buttermilk for tanginess and a touch of vanilla for warmth, Mississippi Pancakes pair perfectly with a drizzle of molasses, maple syrup, or a dollop of whipped honey butter. Whether enjoyed for breakfast or as a treat any time of day, they bring a taste of Southern hospitality to your plate.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 1/4 cup flour
  • 3 teaspoons baking powder
  • 3 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Directions

  1. Heat nonstick griddle over medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, beat eggs until light and foamy. Add milk, oil, and melted butter.
  3. In another bowl, sift together flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt. Beat dry ingredients into the liquid ingredients with a wire whisk until smooth.
  4. When the griddle is hot, pour about 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake.
  5. Cook until bubbles form on the surface of the pancake and the edges start to look set, about 1-2 minutes. Flip and cook until golden brown on the other side.
  6. Serve hot with your favorite toppings.
